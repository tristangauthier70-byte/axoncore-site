# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Singapore SME service-business owners (clinics, dental/GP practices, aesthetics/beauty, real estate, home services) evaluating whether to buy an AI voice/chat receptionist. They typically arrive via ads, referral, or direct search, are often skeptical of "AI hype," and need to trust that this will handle real customer calls and money-generating bookings competently before committing to a 36-month agreement. Not developers, not consumers — busy operators who want proof, not spectacle.

## Product Purpose

axoncoreai.com is the primary sales surface for Axon Core AI, a Singapore-based AI automation agency selling three tiers of AI voice/chat/omnichannel front-desk packages (Package A/B/C, see [[Axon Core/Pricing & Packages]]). Success = visitor books a strategy call or becomes a founding client (first 50 clients get locked-in founding rates). The site must do the credibility-building work a human salesperson would do face-to-face, since the buyer is committing to real recurring revenue for their business.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, intelligent, confident. Quiet-luxury premium — not shouting, not decorated. The "intelligence" of the brand should be felt through a subtle recurring visual motif representing Riley's "brain" (a dark, node-graph/constellation pattern, evoking a neural network without being literal or gimmicky), not through loud color or ornamentation. Confidence comes from generous whitespace, disciplined type hierarchy, and proof (real numbers, real ROI math) rather than hype language.

## Anti-references

- The current site's own purple-saturated look: colored borders separating every section, a purple radial-glow sweep on every section transition, heavy accent-color usage throughout (buttons, headers, dividers, panels) — reads as generic "AI startup" cliché rather than corporate-credible.
- Generic SaaS gradient-hero clichés, side-stripe colored borders, gradient text, glassmorphism-as-decoration, identical icon-card grids repeated section after section, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows on every section, numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding where there's no real sequence.
- Anything that reads as "AI made this" at a glance.

## Design Principles

1. **Whitespace separates, color doesn't.** Sections are divided by generous vertical spacing, never by colored border lines or repeated glow sweeps.
2. **One accent, used sparingly.** The existing violet (#A78BFA family) survives only as a rare, deliberate accent — primary CTAs and select headline words — not a page-wide wash. Base palette goes near-monochrome (black/white/gray).
3. **Show intelligence, don't say it.** The "Riley's brain" node-graph motif and real proof (ROI math, live-feeling data) carry the "smart AI" positioning — not badges, not buzzwords.
4. **Fill empty space with one considered thing, not more clutter.** A live-feeling widget or a single strong visual beats scattering extra copy/icons across a thin section.
5. **Practice what we sell.** As an AI automation company asking SME owners to trust us with their customer calls, the site itself must feel more competently built and cared-for than the sites of the traditional receptionists/generic chatbots we're positioned against.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance as the floor (contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation) — balanced deliberately against visual impact rather than defaulting to the safest possible choice. Where a motion or contrast choice is borderline, resolve it in favor of AA compliance first, then find the most visually striking version that still clears the bar.
